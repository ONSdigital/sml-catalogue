About the Statistical Methods Library

What is the Statistical Methods Library?

A central library containing statistical methods for consistent reproducible statistical methods used within the Office for National Statistics (ONS)

Further information:

A central library for approved statistical methods for use within the ONS and wider public sector. [OR (1) A central library for assured statistical methods for use within the ONS and wider public sector. (2) A central library for governed statistical methods for use within the ONS and wider public sector].

The library ensures that official statistical outputs are fully transparent. Such transparency provides proportionate governance and assurance processes, including statistical production and analysis/exploration through peer reviews. Additionally, continuous improvement is facilitated from such reviews and user feedback, i.e. method change requests and/or new method requests.

Through this library the ONS shares methods of best practice with other government departments. Providing comprehensive statistical method documentation, including but not limited to: (i) catalogue of methods; (ii) method codes and specifications; (iiii) how-to guidance; and (iv) contact details for help and support.
